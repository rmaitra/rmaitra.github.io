Legislative Powers:

The Council creates and modifies laws, policies, and ordinances. They approve the budget working with the Mayor, and can override mayoral vetoes with 9 votes. The Council has purely legislative duties after the 2021 charter amendment shifted executive power to the Mayor.

Independent Authority:

These boards have authority to levy taxes and fees within their areas, subject to limitations set by the Board of Estimate and Taxation. The Park Board is elected by voters citywide and operates semi-independently from the City Council.

Government Structure Overview:

2021 Charter Amendment: Minneapolis voters approved transitioning from a "weak mayor-strong council" to a "strong mayor-legislative council" system. The Mayor now holds executive power and appoints department heads, while the Council has purely legislative duties. This change clarified the separation of powers between branches.

Composition: As of January 2026, the City Council has 12 DFL members and 1 Independent (Robin Wonsley). Four members identify with Democratic Socialists of America (DSA): Aisha Chughtai, Robin Wonsley, Jason Chavez, and Soren Stevenson.
