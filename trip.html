Option 01 · Road Trip

The Atlantic Coastal Dash

Portland, ME → Bar Harbor → Bay of Fundy → Prince Edward Island  ·  ~9–10 hrs driving

Day-by-Day Itinerary
May 7 · Wednesday
Arrival & the Maine Coast
  • Midday
    ✈ Fly into Portland (PWM) and pick up your rental car
    Book your car in advance — May is the start of tourist season
  • 1:00 PM
    🦞 Lobster rolls at The Highroller Lobster Co. on Commercial St.
    Try the Connecticut-style warm butter roll — a regional classic
  • 2:30 PM
    🚗 Scenic drive north on Rte 1 toward Bar Harbor (3 hrs)
    Stop at Rockport or Camden if you have time — stunning harbor towns
  • 6:00 PM
    🌅 Catch the sunset at Sand Beach in Acadia National Park
    The pink granite cliffs glow orange — genuinely surreal light
  • 8:00 PM
    🍺 Dinner on Main St, Bar Harbor — try Café This Way or Havana
    Reservations recommended; Bar Harbor fills up fast in May
May 8 · Thursday
The International Trek
  • 5:00 AM
    🌄 Sunrise at Cadillac Mountain — first place in the US to see the sun
    Reserve a timed entry pass; temps at summit can be near freezing in May
  • 8:00 AM
    🚗 Begin the 4.5-hour drive to New Brunswick via the Canadian border
    Have your passport ready. The crossing at St. Stephen is usually quick
  • 1:30 PM
    🪨 Hopewell Rocks — walk on the ocean floor at low tide
    Check the tide schedule! The rocks are only walkable ~2hrs around low tide
  • 4:00 PM
    🌉 Cross the 8-mile Confederation Bridge into Prince Edward Island
    The only fixed link to the island — a genuinely dramatic drive over open water
  • Evening
    🏡 Check in and explore Charlottetown's waterfront
    PEI time is 1hr ahead of EDT — plan accordingly
May 9 · Friday
PEI Island Life
  • 9:00 AM
    📗 Anne of Green Gables Heritage Place in Cavendish
    Even if you haven't read the books, the restored farmhouse is charming
  • 11:30 AM
    🏖 PEI National Park — red sandstone cliffs and sweeping dunes
    The water is cold in May (10°C) but the beaches are completely empty
  • 2:00 PM
    🚴 Rent bikes and cycle the Confederation Trail — flat & gorgeous
    The trail follows old railway beds through pastoral farmland
  • 6:00 PM
    🦪 Oyster dinner in Charlottetown — try The Lobster on the Wharf
    Malpeque Bay oysters are world-famous. Order by the dozen.
  • 8:30 PM
    🎻 Live Celtic fiddle music at The Old Triangle Irish Alehouse
    PEI has a strong Acadian/Celtic heritage — this is the real deal
May 10 · Saturday
The Long Haul Home
  • 6:00 AM
    🌅 Early departure — pack coffee and a breakfast sandwich
    9+ hour drive: break it at Moncton (NB) and then again at Bangor (ME)
  • En route
    🍁 Quick stop at Fundy Trail Parkway for coastal views
    Worth 30 minutes — dramatic cliffs over the Bay of Fundy
  • 3:00 PM
    🦞 Final Maine lobster stop — Red's Eats in Wiscasset
    Legendary lobster roll. Expect a line but it moves fast.
  • 5:00 PM
    ✈ Return car and depart from Portland (PWM)
    Book evening flights out — the drive leaves no buffer for early departures

Option 02 · Train Journey

The French-Canadian Escape

Montréal → VIA Rail → Québec City  ·  Classic, walkable, European feeling

Day-by-Day Itinerary
May 7 · Wednesday
Welcome to Montréal
  • Midday
    ✈ Fly into Montréal-Trudeau (YUL) and head to Old Montreal
    Take the 747 express bus from the airport — quick and cheap
  • 2:00 PM
    ⛪ Visit the Notre-Dame Basilica — stunning Gothic Revival interior
    Book the AURA light show (evening) for a jaw-dropping experience
  • 4:00 PM
    🧱 Walk the cobblestone streets of Vieux-Montréal and the waterfront
    Rue Saint-Paul is the oldest commercial street in North America
  • 6:00 PM
    🍟 Poutine at La Banquise — open 24/7, over 30 varieties
    Try the "T-Rex" — fries, gravy, cheese curds, and pulled pork
  • Night
    🍸 Explore the cocktail scene on Rue Saint-Denis or Plateau area
    Montréal has one of the best bar cultures in North America
May 8 · Thursday
Mount Royal & The Train
  • 8:30 AM
    🌲 Hike up Mount Royal Park to the Kondiaronk Belvedere lookout
    45-min walk up; or take the shuttle in May if the road is open
  • 11:00 AM
    ☕ Brunch at Olive + Gourmando in Old Montreal before the train
    Best avocado toast in the city — worth the short line
  • 1:30 PM
    🚂 Board the VIA Rail at Gare Centrale — 3 scenic hours to Québec City
    Upgrade to Business Class (~$40 extra) for wide seats, wine, and snacks
  • 4:30 PM
    🏰 Arrive in Québec City, check in, explore Petit-Champlain
    The funicular connects Upper and Lower Town — a fun ride
  • 7:00 PM
    🍷 Dinner in the Petit Champlain district — the most beautiful street in Canada
    Try Lapin Sauté for French-Canadian game dishes in a cozy bistro setting
May 9 · Friday
Québec City Charm
  • 9:00 AM
    🏰 Tour the Château Frontenac — book a guided history tour inside
    The most photographed hotel in the world, perched over the St. Lawrence
  • 10:30 AM
    🚶 Walk the Dufferin Terrace boardwalk for panoramic St. Lawrence views
    Walk to the Plains of Abraham — site of the 1759 Battle of Quebec
  • 1:00 PM
    💦 Montmorency Falls — taller than Niagara, only 15 min from the city
    Take the cable car to the top and walk across the suspension bridge
  • 4:00 PM
    🛍 Browse the art galleries and boutiques of Rue Saint-Jean
    Great for local maple products, ice wine, and Québécois folk art
  • 7:00 PM
    🍖 Traditional Québécois feast at Aux Anciens Canadiens
    Housed in one of the oldest buildings in Québec City (1675). Get the tourtière.
May 10 · Saturday
Last Crêpes & Departure
  • 8:00 AM
    🥐 Breakfast at Paillard — best croissants, brioche, and coffee in QC
    Arrive early. The pastry case sells out quickly on weekends.
  • 10:00 AM
    🚂 Morning train back to Montréal (3 hrs) — OR fly from YQB
    Jean Lesage Airport (YQB) has direct connections — check if it saves time
  • En route
    📖 Read, relax, and enjoy the St. Lawrence River valley scenery
    This is the payoff for choosing the train — you arrive refreshed
  • Afternoon
    ✈ Afternoon or evening flights home from YUL
    Build in 2.5 hrs before departure — Trudeau airport can be slow

Side-by-Side Comparison

Which escape suits your group? Here's an honest breakdown.

The Atlantic Coastal Dash

Option 01 · Road Trip

Vibe

Rugged, salty-air adventure. Sea spray, lobster shacks, and open highways. Feels like a bucket-list road trip.

Effort Level

High. Long days of driving, early morning hikes, and a 9-hour return push. You'll need energy.

Scenery

Spectacular and varied: pink granite seacoasts, ocean floor tides, red sand cliffs, and pastoral island farms.

Food & Culture

Seafood-centric. Lobster, Malpeque oysters, chowder, and fiddlehead ferns. Acadian Celtic heritage.

Logistics

Requires a rental car for the whole trip. Passport required for Canada. Tidal timing at Hopewell Rocks is critical.

Group Size Fit

Great for 3 (splits car costs). Works best if everyone is comfortable with long drives and flexible spontaneity.

Total Per Person

~$1,350 incl. flight

vs

The French-Canadian Escape

Option 02 · Train Journey

Vibe

Sophisticated, cosmopolitan, "pretend you're in Europe." Cobblestones, wine on the train, world-class food.

Effort Level

Low to medium. The train does the work. Days are full but never exhausting. Perfect for a relaxing group trip.

Scenery

Classic and gorgeous: Old World architecture, St. Lawrence River valley, waterfalls, and the UNESCO-worthy walled city.

Food & Culture

Outstanding. French-Canadian cuisine, world-class brunch culture, tourtière, maple everything, and great cocktail bars.

Logistics

Very straightforward. Fly in, walk everywhere in Montréal, board the train, walk everywhere in Québec City. No car needed.

Group Size Fit

Works for any group size. No coordination of drivers or car costs. Everyone can relax and stay together easily.

Total Per Person

~$1,370 incl. flight

✦ Our Recommendation
Go French-Canadian.

For a 4-day group trip in May, Option 2 is the smarter choice. The Atlantic Coastal Dash is an incredible adventure — but it's a grueling one. You'd spend nearly half of your 4 days just driving, arrive home exhausted, and be at the mercy of tide tables and long border crossings. Option 2 delivers maximum delight per hour of effort: two of the most beautiful cities in North America, incredible food, a scenic train ride with a glass of wine, and you return home feeling genuinely refreshed. The costs are nearly identical, making this an easy call.

That said: if your group specifically lives for road trips, hates cities, and someone already has TSA PreCheck + Canadian border pass — Option 1 is a once-in-a-lifetime run.
