Minnesota State Government

Minnesota has a rich tradition of government service and public administration. The state's government structure is designed to serve its diverse population efficiently and effectively.

Three Branches of Government

Executive Branch

The executive branch is led by the Governor and includes various state agencies and departments that carry out state laws and provide services to Minnesotans.

  • Governor's Office
  • Lieutenant Governor
  • State Agencies and Departments

Legislative Branch

The Minnesota Legislature consists of two bodies:

  • Senate: 67 members serving 4-year terms
  • House of Representatives: 134 members serving 2-year terms

Judicial Branch

Minnesota's court system ensures justice and interprets laws. It includes:

  • Minnesota Supreme Court
  • Court of Appeals
  • District Courts

Key Facts

Minnesota became the 32nd state to join the Union on May 11, 1858.

The state capital is Saint Paul, while Minneapolis is the largest city. Together, they form the "Twin Cities" metropolitan area.

Government Services

Minnesota state government provides numerous services including:

  1. Education and early childhood programs
  2. Healthcare services and public health
  3. Transportation infrastructure
  4. Environmental protection
  5. Public safety and corrections
  6. Economic development and workforce programs

For more information, visit the official Minnesota state website at mn.gov.
