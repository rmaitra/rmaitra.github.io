City of Minneapolis, Minnesota Draft — For Deliberation
Plug-In Solar Photovoltaic Device
A draft ordinance relating to energy; regulating the installation and operation of a plug-in solar photovoltaic device; proposing new provisions in the Minneapolis Code of Ordinances.

The City of Minneapolis finds that portable plug-in solar photovoltaic devices provide a low-barrier means for residents—including renters and low-to-moderate income households—to participate in clean energy generation, consistent with the City's Climate Equity Plan and its goal of 100% renewable electricity by 2030. This ordinance removes unnecessary regulatory obstacles to the adoption of these devices.

Be it ordained by the City Council of the City of Minneapolis:

Section 1. [Minneapolis Code of Ordinances §XXX.XX] — Plug-In Solar Photovoltaic Device

Subdivision 1. Definitions. For the purposes of this ordinance, the following terms have the meanings given:

  • (a)"Energy storage system" has the meaning given in Minnesota Statutes, section 216B.2422, subdivision 1.
  • (b)"Photovoltaic device" has the meaning given in Minnesota Statutes, section 216C.06, subdivision 16.
  • (c) "Plug-in solar photovoltaic device" means a portable photovoltaic device that:
    • (1)is intended primarily to offset a portion of a customer's electricity consumption;
    • (2)has a maximum power output of 1,200 watts;
    • (3)is capable of being connected with an on-site energy storage system; and
    • (4)is listed or certified as compliant with the requirements for interactive plug-in photovoltaic equipment and systems by a testing laboratory that is recognized under the federal Occupational Health and Safety Agency's Nationally Recognized Testing Laboratory program to certify photovoltaic equipment and systems.

Subd. 2. Exemptions.

  • (a) A plug-in solar photovoltaic device is exempt from:
    • (1)any City permit, fee, or approval requirement related to the device's installation or operation, provided the device is not permanently affixed to the structure;
    • (2)a requirement to enter an interconnection agreement with an electric utility; and
    • (3)any fee, condition, approval, or reporting requirement that an electric utility imposes with respect to the plug-in solar photovoltaic device's installation or operation.
  • (b)An electric utility is not liable for damage or injury caused by a plug-in solar photovoltaic device.

Subd. 3. Renter Rights. No landlord or property owner subject to the Minneapolis Rent Stabilization Ordinance or the Renters' Protection Ordinance shall prohibit a tenant from installing or operating a plug-in solar photovoltaic device on a balcony, patio, or private outdoor space associated with the tenant's unit, provided the device meets the definition in Subdivision 1(c) and does not obstruct fire egress or violate applicable safety codes.

Subd. 4. Utility Coordination. The City of Minneapolis shall formally request that Xcel Energy and relevant state regulators adopt interconnection standards for plug-in solar photovoltaic devices that are consistent with the exemptions set forth in Subdivision 2, including the elimination of interconnection fees and streamlined technical requirements equivalent to those established under California Senate Bill 868.

Effective Date. This ordinance is effective 30 days following publication after final adoption by the Minneapolis City Council.
