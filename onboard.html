üîç Where to Find Agency Information

1. Official Agency Websites

Every state agency has its own website with detailed information about their programs and services.

  • Main Portal: MN.gov Visit mn.gov to find links to all state agencies
  • Agency URLs Most follow the pattern: agency-name.state.mn.us or mn.gov/agency-name
  • Example: Department of Health: health.state.mn.us

2. Minnesota State Directory

The Secretary of State publishes an annual directory of all state agencies, commissioners, and contact information.

üí° Pro Tip: Search "Minnesota Blue Book" or "Minnesota State Agency Directory" to find the official list of all agencies with their current leadership and phone numbers.

3. Open Meeting Portal

  • Public Meetings Calendar Many agencies post their meeting schedules and agendas online. Check individual agency websites for "Meetings" or "Calendar" sections.

üì∞ How to Stay Informed

  1. Subscribe to Agency Newsletters
    Most agencies offer email newsletters with updates on programs, rule changes, and public meetings. Look for "Subscribe" or "Email Updates" on agency websites.
  2. Follow on Social Media
    Many agencies maintain active Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn accounts with real-time updates.
  3. Check News & Press Releases
    Agencies publish press releases about major announcements. Find them under "News" or "Press Room" sections.
  4. Review Annual Reports
    Agencies publish yearly reports detailing their activities, accomplishments, and budgets. Search for "Annual Report" on agency websites.
  5. Monitor Rulemaking
    When agencies create new rules, they must hold public comment periods. Visit the State Register at revisor.mn.gov to see proposed rules.
